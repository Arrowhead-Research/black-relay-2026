What is black relay?

A technical systems-integration event. Not a generic hackathon.

No obligations

Black Relay is not a full-time commitment. Work is asynchronous and remote. Replace your homelab time or side-project commitments with Black Relay.

Add to your portfolio

In a timeframe of approximately four months, the goal is to build a fieldable subsystems with documentation, interfaces, and test evidence.

Work on a real problem

The final system should be deployable in an unfamiliar building with minimal setup and continue functioning when connectivity or individual components fail.

Network with like-minded talent

The program culminates in a live, in-person integration and deployment phase with travel paid for by Arrowhead Research.

The challenge

Rapidly emplace a portable safehouse system under realistic constraints.

How can a small team provide local awareness, detect compromise or degradation, preserve essential functions, and support operator decisions during a multi-day stress period?

Understand

Turn distributed sensors into useful site context and prioritized alerts.

Survive

Keep critical compute, power, network, and reporting pathways visible under disruption.

Decide

Present degradation, risk, and recommended operator actions without overwhelming the user.

Build rhythm

Four remote months, then live deployment and stress testing.

  1. Month 1 — Requirements and ArchitectureDefine subsystem concepts, interfaces, assumptions, and integration points.
  2. Month 2 — PrototypingBuild early subsystem prototypes and validate technical feasibility.
  3. Month 3 — Cross-Team IntegrationConnect subsystems, refine interfaces, discover failure modes, and iterate.
  4. Month 4 — Hardening and RehearsalImprove reliability, documentation, installation workflow, and demo readiness.
  5. In-Person Event — Deployment and Stress TestingInstall, operate, and evaluate the integrated safehouse stack in a live setting.

Success criteria

Credible. Modular. Survivable. Operator-usable.

Installs quickly in unfamiliar structures Continues under degraded conditions Detects tampering or failure Preserves critical information Presents useful decisions to operators Uses clear interfaces and documentation

Frequently Asked Questions

Do I have to be in the military?

Black Relay participants must be either active military or actively drilling members of the national guard or reserves.

However, if you are a veteran or a member of industry looking to support, we can bring you on as an advisor for the project; acting as an observer, coach and mentor to the participants.

What if I don't have any experience?

You don't have to be an expert, but Black Relay is not a boot camp. The expectation is that you can provide meaningful contributions to your team.

Who owns the IP?

Black Relay is built in the open and will be open sourced under the MIT license. The goal of black relay is not to produce something that can be productized, but to produce an experience that meaningfully engages and develops engineering talent within the military.

I don't think I'll have the time. Can I still participate?

We understand that life happens and that this is a volunteer event. Black Relay does not take precedence over family matters or your day job. We expect participants to be able to commit at least a few hours per week to the project to provide meaningful contributions.
